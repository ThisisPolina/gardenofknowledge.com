From Polina Parfenova, A first-year International Business Student, student id: 68628320

Q: What do you consider must-know things as a student?
A: I think students should know that you will need to participate in a lot of events because of activity points, which are required for graduating. I also think that students should know about the library, and that you can watch movies there with your friends once you get a student ID card. Also, you will be able to get discounts in a couple of places if you show them your student ID card. There is also a nurse’s office where they provide a lot of medicine for free, especially if you’re on your period, hurt your leg, or are experiencing extreme allergies. They mostly speak Thai, but you can use Google Translate or ask your Thai classmate to help you.
Q: What do you consider must-do things as a student?
A: Make sure to do your 90-day report on time, visa extension on time, and get a driver’s license as soon as possible. If you own a motorbike, don’t forget to change your oil and check your tires to avoid issues.
Q: What important items should students bring from their home country that are difficult to find here?
A: Although I don’t own any traditional clothes from my country, I would encourage others to bring theirs because we have a lot of cultural events, and many people take pride in how good they look in them.
Q: How do internships and research projects work in 3rd and 4th year?
A: I don’t know how internships work, but I heard that getting a bad review from your employer will force you to redo the internship. I have heard about the research project briefly. All I know is that it will account for a majority of our grade.
Q: What do students mean when they say studying at CMRU gets harder? Does it differ by major?
A: I heard that we will have to answer orally, on the spot, and complete more assignments on-site instead of as homework.
Q: How did you find affordable accommodation?
A: I live with my family, but I heard from my classmates that they find places online or through friends.
Q: How can students take additional subjects?
A: I think you need to ask your advisor first and make sure to avoid schedule conflicts.
Q: If you live in a dorm, how did you secure a spot, and what is it like living in one?
A: I don’t live in a dorm. I heard that you’ll need to share a room with a bunch of people, which is not ideal for me.
Q: Where do you usually shop for school clothing, regular clothing, groceries, and daily necessities?
A: I shop at Makro for groceries because it’s close to my home. I wish I had gotten the membership sooner because I go there often. I wish I had known about Hola Hola Secondhand Clothing because the clothes there are cheap, but it can take a long time to find something that suits my taste. I bought my school skirt at Kad Na Mor, but I heard they also sell school clothing near CMRU. I also bought my button-up shirts at Lotus for 100 baht each, and they are 100% cotton, which is ideal for me because I sweat a lot.
Q: What are or were your favorite subjects and why?
A: I like Statistics because it’s easier than I expected (for now).
Q: What are or were your favorite student activities or experiences, and why?
A: I liked International Day because it was great to see people perform. I also liked the Water Festival because it was a lot of fun shooting people with a water gun and watching them dance to songs. I also really enjoyed participating in the Paracycling Liaison Officer event because I met amazing people and learned problem-solving skills.
Q: What do you wish you had known about your major and year beforehand?
A: I wish I had known that it is okay to miss class if you have a leave letter and are participating in an event. I wish I had known that you cannot see your grades immediately and will have to wait some time to receive your midterm and final scores. I wish I had known that some students are shy or have difficulties with projects because they don’t know what to do and are too shy to ask.
Q: What is your dress code? How does it change over the years?
A: My dress code is a button-up white shirt and a black skirt or pants. Sneakers are okay. We can wear activity shirts, and some Ajarns are okay with us wearing casual clothes. I know that the dress code will get stricter because they want us to appear more professional.
Q: What should students in your major and year prepare before starting?
A: I think it’s necessary to download Google Translate and Teams because we do our assignments on it, Line because everyone communicates there, Bolt for getting around the city, and a voice-recording app in case your friends miss a class or you would like to review the lesson. Sunscreen is important because it can get quite hot here in Thailand. A steamer or an iron is also useful for making your clothes look nice, especially if you study at ABM. A toothbrush and hairbrush are also useful for making sure your photos turn out well during events.
Q: What does your typical workload or school day look like?
A: My easy days are when we don’t have a class and just need to read and do homework. My normal days involve listening to a lecture, then completing an assignment in class. Sometimes, we can do it as homework instead. My difficult days involve working on posters in a group and/or having a lot of homework and projects.
Q: How has your experience changed across semesters or years academically?
A: I’m a first-year student. Although I considered many subjects to be easy in the first semester, I’m enjoying the second semester because I am more efficient with my time and less stressed since I already know what to expect.
Q: Any additional advice, tips, or comments for future students?
A: I think it’s important for future students to check Line and Facebook often because they can miss important information or events. I would recommend signing up as soon as possible because some spots are limited. They usually choose based on who signed up first. Also, please set up reminders so you don’t forget to do your homework or attend class because schedules can change. I also wish that other students would try their hardest on free-choice questions because you can still get points for effort.
