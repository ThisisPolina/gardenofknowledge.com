From an anonymous first-year International Business Student

Q: What do you consider must-know things as a student?
A: As a student, we have to know how to register our study plan for each semester, how to borrow books at the library, and know every place in CMRU.
Q: What do you consider must-do things as a student?
A: Do your 90-day report and visa extension, and pay monthly rent before the due date.
Q: What important items should students bring from their home country that are difficult to find here?
A: I recommend them to bring their traditional clothes from their country and/or their local spices.
Q: How do internships and research projects work in 3rd and 4th year?
A: I'm just a first-year student, but it looks like hard work because we have to do it very clearly so students don't need to redo the internship. Make sure you understand what the employer ordered you to do, and you have to apply what you have learned in class.
Q: What do students mean when they say studying at CMRU gets harder? Does it differ by major?
A: I've never heard that studying at CMRU is hard.
Q: Where do you usually shop for school clothing, regular clothing, groceries, and daily necessities?
A: Usually, I go to Siri Wattana Market to buy groceries and uniforms. For my daily necessities, I go to Jumbo Store or Beauty Mart, and Shopee to buy miscellaneous things which have cheaper prices than offline stores.
Q: What are or were your favorite subjects and why?
A: Accounting and Statistics, because I like numbers.
Q: What are or were your favorite student activities or experiences, and why?
A: I liked International Day because we can explore our friends' cultures.
Q: What do you wish you had known about your major and year beforehand?
A: I wish I had known that it's okay to miss class if we have something important to do, like extending our student visa.
Q: What is your dress code? How does it change over the years?
A: We can wear activity shirts for in-class study, but we have to wear uniforms for attending midterm exams and final exams.
Q: What should students in your major and year prepare before starting?
A: We should prepare gadgets (iPad or laptop, phone) or books to support our studies.
Q: What does your typical workload or school day look like?
A: Easy days are when Ajarns keep lecturing without any assignments. Normal days are when Ajarns give us assignments at the same time. Difficult days are when we have to do some group projects.
Q: How has your experience changed across semesters or years academically?
A: I have no complaints because I'm enjoying my time as a student.
Q: Any additional advice, tips, or comments for future students?
A: You should check the International College Facebook Page or website to get the latest info.
