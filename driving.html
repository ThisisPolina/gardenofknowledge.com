Driving in Chiang Mai

Q: Why motorbike?
A: The design of many routes and roads is more convenient for motorbikes than for cars. Driving a motorbike will be faster and helps you avoid being late to class. It’s also easier to find parking.
Popular brands are Scoopy, Fino, Filano, and Wave. Automatic motorbikes are very common.
Q: How to buy a motorbike?
A: Renting a motorbike is more expensive than buying one. There are many motorbike shops, but students mostly buy online on Facebook Marketplace or from someone they know. When buying a motorbike, it is essential that the seller provides you with a Green Book.
Q:Where to buy motorbike?
Many students use Facebook Marketplace to buy motorbikes.

Sometimes motorbikes are sold without a Green Book, which is very risky because you won’t be able to legally transfer ownership or sell it later. Some listings don’t show the Green Book, but you can always ask the seller.
Many students also arrange shipping from another city. It is recommended to check the motorbike in person when possible. High mileage is not necessarily bad if the motorbike is well maintained, but lower mileage is usually preferred.
Q: Do I need a helmet?
Yes. Please buy a helmet for your safety and to avoid fines. A full-face helmet is recommended and usually costs around 900 baht.

Q: How to take care of a motorbike?
It is important to go to a mechanic who can check the condition of your motorbike. Pump your tires at the gas station regularly to avoid issues. You will also need to change the engine oil every 1000–1500 km if you use basic oil. If this is ignored for too long, it may cause serious engine damage that is expensive to fix.
Q: How to pay road tax?
Bring your Green Book and passport. You will need to have your motorbike inspected, then buy insurance, and finally pay the road tax.
